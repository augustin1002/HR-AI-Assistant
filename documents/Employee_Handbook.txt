Adino Telecom Ltd.
THE EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK

1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 Purpose of the handbook
This Adino Telecom Ltd. Employee Handbook establishes policies, procedures, benefits, and working conditions that will be followed by all employees as a condition of their employment at the Company. The Standards of Conduct describe the expected actions and behaviours of employees while conducting Company business. This Employee Handbook is not a contract of employment. The Company reserves the right to change or withdraw policies at any time.

1.2 Welcome message from the CEO
Welcome to Adino Telecom Ltd. Each employee contributes directly to the Company's growth and success. - Somnath Singha, CEO.

1.3 Company history
Adino Telecom Ltd. is India's premier broadband solutions company established in 1992, a joint venture between Onida Group and Enkay Telecom Communications Ltd. It provides wireless communications, broadband, and radio communication solutions across India, serving sectors including Police, Railways, Defense, Cement, Oil, Steel, and Irrigation departments. Adino is an exclusive distributor for ICOM, Japan.

1.4 Company Mission
To achieve Customer Satisfaction and maintain excellent Customer Relations by providing high quality Wireless Communication and Networking Solutions and to foster a strong and competent work force within the organization.

1.5 Company Vision
Expansion of market reach, tapping wider customer base, introducing new products, tapping global markets, and achieving excellence in vendor management.

1.6 Company Values
Respect for individuals, encouraging passion and perfection, integrity, attention to detail, "Achieving the impossible" motto, and strong customer relationships.

1.7 Company commitment to employees
Fair wages, training and skill development, labor practices around human rights, respect for right of association, and work/life balance with flexible working opportunities.

1.8 Business ethics and code of conduct
Core principles: Honesty, Integrity, Trustworthiness, Loyalty, Fairness, Concern for others, Respect for others, Commitment to Excellence, Leadership, Reputation and Morale, Accountability. Includes policy on compliance with law, confidentiality of information, and safeguarding company property.

1.9 Confidentiality Policy
Employees must maintain confidentiality of proprietary and confidential information including customer lists, financial data, and personnel files. Violations result in disciplinary action and possible legal recourse. Employee data will not be released to outside parties without authorization except when compelled by court order.

2. GENERAL EMPLOYMENT INFORMATION

2.1 Employment Eligibility
Minimum age 18 years. Minimum qualification HSC or equivalent.

2.2 Joining Information
Employees submit a Joining Report with residence proof, birth certificate, educational certificates, previous employer certificates, last 2 months salary slips, PAN card, and photographs, followed by an induction process.

2.3 Internal Employee Application Process
Employees can apply for internal postings after 6 months in current position, subject to performance standards, no recent disciplinary action, and meeting job qualifications. Process includes application, supervisor approval, HR submission, and interviews.

2.4 Appointment and Probationary Process
Categories: New Hire (6-12 month probation), Rehiring, Transfers, Promotions (6-12 month trial), Demotions. Probation for new hires is 6 months, evaluated at 2nd and 5th months, extendable if needed.

2.5 Employment Categories
Regular full-time employees, Probation employees, Contractual employees, Casual employees, Management Trainees (1-3 months, unpaid, may be absorbed).

2.6 Promotions
Based on demonstrated ability and job performance; internal candidates considered first, outside recruiting also used when needed.

2.7 Open Door Policy
Employees may discuss concerns with any manager at any level or with HR, encouraging open communication and feedback.

2.8 Rehiring Policy
Former employees rehired after more than 1 year are treated as introductory employees for 90 days; benefits may or may not carry over. Rehire after less than 1 year reinstates prior benefit levels.

2.9 Referral Policy
Employees referring candidates receive benefits after 3 months of the referred candidate's tenure: Senior Management Rs.3000, Middle Management Rs.2000, Junior Level Rs.1000.

2.10 Management Trainee Policy
Students from management schools recruited for 1-3 months, project based, unpaid, may be absorbed if competent.

2.11 Personnel File Policy
Personnel files are confidential and maintained by HR, containing applications, disciplinary history, resumes, and personal information. Employees may view but not alter their file, only in presence of HR staff.

2.12 Access to Personnel Records
Employees may view certain contents (application, promotion, disciplinary, transfer records, training records) with 24-hour notice, in presence of HR. References, investigation records, and medical records are excluded.

3. ATTENDANCE AT WORK

3.1 Working Hours
Office hours: 9:00 am to 5:45 pm or 9:30 am to 6:15 pm. Late arrival by more than 15 minutes is disciplinary; 3+ late marks in a week counts as a half day.

3.2 Break and Lunch Hours
Lunch time is 1:00 pm to 1:30 pm; frequent breaks are discouraged.

3.3 Attendance Expectations and Policies
Emergency personal time accrues at 2 hours, 3 times per month (72 hours annually). Employees must notify supervisors of absence within 60 minutes of shift start.

3.4 Telecommuting Policy
Telecommuting requires manager approval and at least 1 week advance notice; generally limited to 1 day per week or 3 days per month.

4. WORKPLACE PROFESSIONALISM AND COMPANY REPRESENTATION

4.1 Work Dress Code
Business casual standard; semi-formal attire generally required; casual dressing allowed on Fridays and Saturdays.

4.2 Workplace Environment Policy
Smoke-free environment inside all facilities; alcohol and drug-free workplace policy in effect.

4.4 Prohibitions and penalties
Prohibited: excessive tardiness, failure to notify absence, insubordination, rude/abusive language, dishonesty, theft. Penalties depend on severity, up to termination.

4.5 Workplace Visitors
Visitors require prior appointment and must be met in reception/discussion areas; employees must not discuss confidential company information with outsiders.

4.6 Giving and Receiving Gifts and Gratuities
Staff are prohibited from giving/receiving gifts from business partners, except office stationery and calendars.

5. USE OF COMPANY EQUIPMENT AND ELECTRONICS

5.1 Telephone Use
Personal calls allowed only during breaks; work phones are for company business.

5.2 Cell Phone Policy
Prohibited to use cell phones while driving company vehicles/devices; phones should be left at desk during meetings.

5.3 Company Tools, Equipment and Supplies
All electronic equipment and stored information is company property; unauthorized software installation and copying is prohibited.

5.4 Computer and Internet Use Policy
Internet and email are for company business only; accessing pornographic or non-business sites is prohibited and may lead to termination. Company owns all email/computer content.

5.5 Blogging and Social Media Policy
Employees must identify as employees and clarify views are their own if blogging about the company; may not disclose confidential information or use company trademarks without permission.

5.6 Return of Company Property
On termination, employees must return all company property and confidential materials, and certify compliance within 20 days.

6. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS AND EVALUATION

6.1 Performance Management System
Performance Appraisal conducted every 6 months based on KRAs set with Department Heads; incentives disbursed based on appraisal results.

6.2 Employee Conduct and Performance
Employees expected to report on schedule, perform duties diligently, maintain qualifications, demonstrate respect, and use resources judiciously.

6.3 Conflict Resolution
Employees must avoid activities that conflict with business interests or harm company reputation.

6.4 Complaint Procedure
HR Personnel receive complaints regarding privacy or policy violations and conduct investigations.

6.5 Employee Misconduct
Inappropriate or unprofessional conduct is cause for discipline up to immediate termination, at company's sole discretion.

7. OPERATING POLICIES

7.1 Travel for Business Policy
Domestic and international travel requires CEO approval; reimbursement limits vary by employee grade for air/train class, lodging, and boarding.

7.2 Mobile Reimbursement
Monthly amounts allotted by grade: M5/M4 Rs.1500, M3/M2 Rs.1250, M1/M0 Rs.800, O1/O2 Rs.420, A1-A3 Rs.300.

7.3 Conveyance Reimbursement
Maximum monthly claims vary by level, e.g. up to DM level Rs.1875, Manager Rs.2200, Sr. Manager/DGM Rs.2575, GM with car Rs.6000.

7.4 Internet Charges
Rs.300 per month applicable only for Grade M5.

7.5 Training policy
Regular training and development programs, either manager-assigned or self-initiated by employees.

7.6 Business/Sales Call
Employees must maintain a Customer Visit Report (CVR) for business calls, verified before expense claims.

7.7 Deputation Policy
Employees may be deputed to another department/region for up to 3 months with pre-approval from Business Head and CEO; entitled to accommodation, travel, and daily allowance benefits.

7.8 Relocation and Transfer
Types: request, regular, or disciplinary transfer. Leave for relocation up to 3 days; travel and lodging compensation provided.

7.9 Employment Termination
Categories: Dismissal (misconduct or 15+ days absconding), Resignation (with notice period), Discharge (ill-health/disability, one month's basic salary), Voluntary Retirement Scheme.

7.10 Exit Interviews
Exit process requires returning company assets, submitting customer contact lists, department sign-offs, and an exit interview with HR.

8. PAYROLL INFORMATION

8.1 Compensation Schedule
Salary paid in first week of following month. LTA claimable once per financial year. Annual bonus of Rs.3800 disbursed at Diwali. Performance incentives disbursed once yearly. Medical coverage under Star Health Insurance. Investment Plans due by September; proofs due by end of January.

9. BENEFITS

9.1 Provident Fund
Employee contributes 12% of Basic Salary (plus optional VPF); Employer contributes 12% of Basic Salary.

9.2 Gratuity
Paid per Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, at 15 days salary per completed year of service after 5+ years of continuous service.

9.3 Mediclaim / Medical Insurance
Star Healthclaim Medical policy for self, spouse, and child up to Rs.1 lakh.

9.4 ESIC
Applicable to employees earning up to Rs.10,000/month. Employee contributes 1.75% of salary; Employer contributes 4.75%.

9.5 Bonus
Fixed Rs.3840 disbursed to all employees during Diwali.

9.6 Retirement
Retirement age is 58 years.

10. EMPLOYEE LEAVE POLICIES

10.1 Leave application process
Collect leave form from HR, obtain reporting manager approval, and submit via email with a copy to the manager.

10.2 Attendance Policy
Employees must call manager before start time if unable to report; 3 consecutive unnotified absent days is considered voluntary termination.

10.3 Sick Leave
7 days per annum, carried forward up to 21 days then lapses, cannot be encashed, may be merged with Privilege Leave in special cases.

10.4 Privilege leave
25 days per annum, carried forward up to 90 days after which it can be encashed; prefix/suffix applicable.

10.5 Casual leave
8 days per annum, encashed at end of financial year.

10.6 LTA
Available once per financial year; requires confirmation status and at least 3 days of Earned Leave; no cash advance, reimbursed only against bills; can be availed twice in a 4-year block tax-free with proof of journey.
